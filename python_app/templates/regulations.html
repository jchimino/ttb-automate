TTB Label Verification

Regulatory Reference

Key requirements from 27 CFR Parts 4, 5, and 7

Part 4: Wine

Labeling and Advertising of Wine (27 CFR Part 4)

4.30 - General Requirements

  • Brand name must be prominently displayed
  • Class and type of wine required
  • Alcohol content required for wines over 14% ABV
  • Net contents in metric measure

4.39 - Sulfite Declaration

'Contains Sulfites' required if sulfur dioxide exceeds 10 ppm

Part 5: Distilled Spirits

Labeling and Advertising of Distilled Spirits (27 CFR Part 5)

5.32 - Mandatory Label Information

  • Brand name prominently on brand label
  • Class and type designation (e.g., Vodka, Bourbon)
  • Alcohol content as % alc. by vol.
  • Net contents in metric measure
  • Name and address of distiller/bottler/importer

5.35 - Alcohol Content Standards

Tolerance: ±0.3% for products 100 proof or less

Part 7: Malt Beverages

Labeling and Advertising of Malt Beverages (27 CFR Part 7)

7.22 - Mandatory Label Information

  • Brand name on principal display panel
  • Class designation (Ale, Lager, Stout, etc.)
  • Name and address of brewer or importer
  • Net contents
  • Country of origin if imported

7.25 - Alcohol Content

Optional by federal law (state laws may require). Tolerance: ±0.15% if stated

Government Warning (27 CFR 16.21 - ABLA)

Required health warning on all alcoholic beverages

GOVERNMENT WARNING: (1) According to the Surgeon General, women should not drink alcoholic beverages during pregnancy because of the risk of birth defects. (2) Consumption of alcoholic beverages impairs your ability to drive a car or operate machinery, and may cause health problems.

  • Must appear on label affixed to container
  • Must be separate from other information
  • Must be readily legible
  • Exact wording as specified
